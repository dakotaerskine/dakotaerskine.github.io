I'm an undergraduate student studying Computer Science at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. My academic background includes advanced coursework in computer graphics, algorithms, and mathematics as well as research in physically-based rendering, where I've explored light transport, material appearance, and the simulation of optical phenomena. I've further broadened my experience through a semester abroad at Nanyang Technological University in Singapore, mentoring roles supporting fellow students in their coursework, and recognition as a member of Upsilon Pi Epsilon.

My work centers on physically-based rendering, with an emphasis on light transport, global illumination, and appearance modeling. I’m particularly interested in how micro and nanoscale surface structures influence the way light interacts with materials.

Beyond the physics of light, I’m drawn to developing rendering techniques that balance physical accuracy with computational efficiency, enabling effects like iridescence, fluorescence, and subsurface scattering to be integrated into production-quality ray tracers.

My work includes exploring novel methods for simulating interference and scattering phenomena, particularly as they apply to realistic appearance models.
